# Calculating Final Pressure Using Boyle's Law

We are given:

- Initial Pressure (P1) = 125 psig  
- Initial Volume (V1) = 25 in³  
- Final Volume (V2) = 50 in³  
- Temperature remains constant  

- Goal: Find final pressure (P2 in psig)

---

## Step 1: Convert gauge pressure to absolute pressure

Boyle's Law requires **absolute pressures**.

    P1_abs = P1 + Atmospheric Pressure
    Atmospheric Pressure ≈ 14.7 psi
    P1_abs = 125 + 14.7 = 139.7 psia

---

## Step 2: Apply Boyle's Law

Boyle’s Law (constant temperature):

    P1_abs × V1 = P2_abs × V2

Solve for P2_abs:

    P2_abs = (P1_abs × V1) / V2

---

## Step 3: Identify the values

- P1_abs = 139.7 psia  
- V1 = 25 in³  
- V2 = 50 in³

---

## Step 4: Apply the formula

    P2_abs = (139.7 × 25) / 50

---

## Step 5: Multiply and divide

    139.7 × 25 = 3,492.5  
    3,492.5 ÷ 50 = 69.85 psia

---

## Step 6: Convert back to gauge pressure

    P2_gauge = P2_abs - Atmospheric Pressure
    P2_gauge = 69.85 - 14.7 ≈ 55.15 psig

---

## Answer

Final Pressure:

    P2 ≈ 55.15 psig
